# ProVAI User Personas

### The Student Persona: "Anna, the Overwhelmed Achiever"

**Who she is:** A diligent, motivated second-year university student. She consistently aims for high marks but often feels overwhelmed by the sheer volume of reading and the abstract nature of complex topics.

**Her Goals:**

-   To find specific, reliable answers to her questions quickly, without getting lost in a 50-page PDF.
-   To check her own understanding in a private, low-stakes environment where she won't be judged.
-   To manage her study time effectively by focusing on the topics that are most important for her upcoming exams.

**Her Pains & Frustrations:**

-   **The "Illusion of Knowing":** She frequently reads a chapter and feels like she understands it, only to realize during an exam that she can't recall or apply the key concepts.
-   **Fear of "Stupid Questions":** She is often hesitant to ask clarifying questions in a large lecture hall or during a professor's limited office hours, fearing she'll look foolish or waste others' time.
-   **Lack of Direction:** She isn't always sure *what* she should be studying or how to connect different concepts from the course material.

**How ProVAI Helps Anna:**

ProVAI is her patient study partner. It provides an instant, non-judgmental space to ask any question, no matter how simple. It helps her defeat the "illusion of knowing" with on-demand, self-assessment quizzes. It gives her a clear, teacher-approved roadmap so she always knows what to focus on next.

> **For Anna, ProVAI's design must be clear, patient, and encouraging.**

---

### The Teacher Persona: "Dr. Evans, the Passionate but Time-Poor Professor"

**Who he is:** An experienced, dedicated professor who is passionate about his subject and genuinely cares about his students' success. He has a high teaching load, large class sizes, and is buried in the administrative tasks that come with modern academia.

**His Goals:**

-   To find a scalable way to provide personalized attention and feedback to all 150 students in his lecture course.
-   To identify which students are falling behind *before* a major exam, when it's still possible to help them.
-   To get objective feedback on which parts of his curriculum are the most confusing for his students, so he can improve his teaching for next semester.

**His Pains & Frustrations:**

-   **Repetitive Workload:** He spends hours every week answering the same five or six fundamental questions via email, which takes time away from helping students with more complex problems.
-   **Lack of Visibility:** He knows from experience that some students are struggling, but he doesn't have the data to know *who* they are or *why*. He feels like he's flying blind until the first midterm grades come in.
-   **No Time for Innovation:** He has great ideas for creating supplementary materials and practice worksheets, but he simply doesn't have the time to produce them.

**How ProVAI Helps Dr. Evans:**

ProVAI is his force multiplier. It automates the handling of repetitive Q&A, freeing him up for higher-value teaching. It gives him a real-time, privacy-preserving "health monitor" for his classroom, flagging confusing topics and disengaged students. It empowers him to generate high-quality support materials with a single click, finally giving him the leverage to act on his innovative teaching ideas.

> **For Dr. Evans, ProVAI's design must be efficient, insightful, and actionable.**
